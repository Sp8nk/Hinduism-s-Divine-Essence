Vedas and Puranas

The "Vedas" and "Puranas" are ancient Indian texts that hold immense significance in Hindu philosophy and culture. The "Vedas", composed in Sanskrit, are the oldest sacred scriptures of Hinduism, believed to have been revealed to sages (rishis).

They consist of four texts: "Rigveda", "Yajurveda", "Samaveda", and "Atharvaveda", encompassing hymns, rituals, and philosophical discourses. The Vedas are revered as "śruti" (that which is heard), representing divine knowledge. They focus on cosmology, metaphysics, and the spiritual laws of the universe, forming the foundation of Hindu thought.

The "Puranas", on the other hand, are a collection of mythological narratives, histories, and ethical teachings written in a later period. They are classified as "smriti" (that which is remembered) and serve to make Vedic ideas accessible to the masses. There are 18 major Puranas, including the "Bhagavata Purana", "Vishnu Purana", and "Shiva Purana", each centered around specific deities and their deeds. These texts explain the creation of the universe, genealogies of gods, kings, and sages, and moral lessons for daily life.

Together, the Vedas and Puranas preserve the spiritual heritage of India, blending ritualistic and devotional traditions while offering timeless guidance on leading a virtuous life.

Vedas

  • Rigveda

    "Oldest Veda, with hymns praising deities, exploring creation, nature, and cosmic principles."

  • Yajurveda

    "Veda of rituals, guiding sacrificial ceremonies with precise mantras and ceremonial instructions."

  • Samaveda

    "Veda of melodies, using Rigvedic hymns in musical form for rituals and devotion."

  • Atharvaveda

    "Veda of knowledge, covering practical life aspects, spells, healing, protection, and spiritual insights."
