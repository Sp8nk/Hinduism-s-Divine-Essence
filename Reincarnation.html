Reincarnation

Reincarnation, or "samsara", is a core belief in Hinduism, referring to the cycle of birth, death, and rebirth that every soul undergoes. Rooted in the idea of the immortal "atman" (soul), this cycle is influenced by karma, the law of cause and effect, which determines the circumstances of each life based on past actions.

Hindu philosophy holds that the soul is eternal and indestructible, merely changing bodies like clothes, as stated in the "Bhagavad Gita". The purpose of reincarnation is to allow the soul to evolve spiritually through experiences, learning, and the fulfillment of "dharma" (moral duty). Each life offers opportunities for growth, addressing unresolved karma, and striving toward self-realization.
The cycle of reincarnation continues until the soul attains "moksha", or liberation, breaking free from "samsara". This ultimate freedom is achieved through self-knowledge, devotion, righteous living, and detachment from material desires.
Reincarnation emphasizes accountability, as current actions shape future lives. It also fosters compassion and humility, encouraging respect for all forms of life. This belief is interwoven with practices like meditation, yoga, and devotion to guide the soul toward liberation and union with the divine.
