Karma

Karma, a central concept in Hinduism, refers to the law of cause and effect governing all actions. Derived from the Sanskrit word meaning "action," karma emphasizes that every thought, word, and deed has consequences, shaping one’s present and future experiences. It operates universally, transcending time, space, and lifetimes, forming a foundational belief in the cycle of birth, death, and rebirth ("samsara").

According to Hindu philosophy, karma is categorized into three types: ""Sanchita"" (accumulated past karma), ""Prarabdha"" (karma influencing the current life), and ""Kriyamana"" (current actions creating future karma). Good deeds ("punya") lead to positive outcomes, fostering spiritual progress, while harmful actions ("papa") result in suffering, necessitating atonement and growth.

Karma is deeply tied to the concept of "dharma" (righteous duty), urging individuals to act selflessly and in alignment with cosmic order. The "Bhagavad Gita" highlights the path of "karma yoga", encouraging detachment from outcomes while performing one’s duties sincerely. This approach purifies the soul, liberating it from the cycle of reincarnation.

Hindu sages teach that understanding karma fosters accountability, compassion, and mindfulness, empowering individuals to shape their destiny through conscious and ethical living, ultimately progressing toward "moksha" (liberation).

Vedas

  • Rigveda

    "Oldest Veda, with hymns praising deities, exploring creation, nature, and cosmic principles."

  • Yajurveda

    "Veda of rituals, guiding sacrificial ceremonies with precise mantras and ceremonial instructions."

  • Samaveda

    "Veda of melodies, using Rigvedic hymns in musical form for rituals and devotion."

  • Atharvaveda

    "Veda of knowledge, covering practical life aspects, spells, healing, protection, and spiritual insights."

Puranas

  • Bhagavata Puran

    "Celebrates Lord Krishna’s life, devotion, spirituality, and the path to liberation through Bhakti."

  • Garuda Puran

    "Discusses afterlife, funeral rites, ethics, cosmology, and sacred knowledge through Lord Vishnu's discourse."

  • Brahma Puran

    "Highlights cosmology, mythology, pilgrimage sites, and rituals associated with Lord Brahma and other deities."

  • Bhavisya Puran

    "Contains prophecies, cultural history, and guidance on religious practices and future events."

  • Agni Puran

    "Focuses on rituals, cosmology, iconography, law, and sacred knowledge of the Vedic tradition."
